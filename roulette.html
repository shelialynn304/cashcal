How roulette is played

Roulette is a wheel game where you place chips on betting spots before the spin. The dealer spins the wheel and drops a ball; wherever the ball lands decides winners and losers.

  • American wheel: 38 pockets (1-36, 0, 00)
  • European wheel: 37 pockets (1-36, 0)
  • After betting closes, no bet changes are allowed
  • Payout depends on how many numbers your bet covers

Roulette rules and bet types

Inside bets target specific numbers (higher payout, lower hit rate). Outside bets cover more outcomes (lower payout, higher hit rate).

  • Straight: 1 number, pays 35:1
  • Dozen/Column: 12 numbers, pays 2:1
  • Red/Black or Odd/Even: 18 numbers, pays 1:1

No matter how you arrange bets, the house edge stays negative for the player over time.

Roulette house edge explained

European roulette house edge: 2.70% (1 zero).

American roulette house edge: 5.26% (0 and 00).

That means expected loss is about $2.70 per $100 wagered on European and $5.26 per $100 wagered on American in the long run.

How betting style changes odds vs risk

Chance to win a spin

Picking outside bets increases hit rate. Picking single numbers lowers hit rate but offers bigger payouts.

Bet size controls volatility

A larger bet does not improve roulette odds. It only increases bankroll swings and raises your bust risk over a session.
