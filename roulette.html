American vs European roulette

European roulette has 37 pockets (1–36 plus 0). American roulette has 38 pockets (1–36 plus 0 and 00).

This extra 00 pocket is a big deal because it nearly doubles the casino advantage on most bets.

  • European house edge: 2.70%
  • American house edge: 5.26%

If both versions are available, choose European. Lower edge means slower expected bankroll drain.

What roulette strategies can and can’t do

Can do: change bet sizing, smooth or amplify swings, and alter short-session outcomes.

Cannot do: turn roulette into a positive expected value game.

Blunt truth: no betting system beats the house edge long-term. Strategy affects volatility, not math edge.

Inside bets vs outside bets

Inside bets cover small groups of numbers and pay higher odds, but hit less often.

  • Straight (1 number): 35:1
  • Split (2 numbers): 17:1
  • Street (3 numbers): 11:1
  • Corner (4 numbers): 8:1
  • Six line (6 numbers): 5:1

Outside bets cover wider ranges and pay less, but hit more often:

  • Red/Black, Odd/Even, High/Low: 1:1
  • Dozens, Columns: 2:1

Why roulette feels streaky

Short runs naturally create clusters. You might see red hit several times in a row or a dozen “go cold.” That feels meaningful, but it usually isn’t.

Human brains over-detect patterns. Variance plus memory bias makes random sequences look less random than they are.

The biggest roulette mistake beginners make

Beginners often raise bet size to “recover losses.” This can create fast drawdowns and emotional decisions.

The core mistake is confusing a short losing streak with a guaranteed rebound. There is no “due” spin.

American vs European roulette: which should you play?

If your goal is maximizing playtime for a fixed bankroll, pick European roulette whenever possible.

At 2.70% edge vs 5.26%, the difference compounds over hundreds of spins and can materially reduce expected losses.

Bankroll rules for roulette

  • Set a fixed entertainment budget before you play.
  • Keep base bets small (often 1% to 2% of session bankroll).
  • Predefine stop-loss and stop-win levels.
  • Never chase losses with emotional bet increases.

Good bankroll rules don’t beat roulette; they help control risk and preserve fun.

Why past spins don’t predict the next spin

Roulette outcomes are independent events. If black hit five times in a row, the next spin still has the same base probability for red/black given the wheel type.

Both the gambler’s fallacy (“red is due”) and hot-hand fallacy (“black stays hot”) can lead to poor decisions.

Roulette FAQ

Is roulette beatable with strategy?

No. Betting systems change short-term variance and risk, but they do not remove the built-in house edge.

Which roulette table is better for players?

European roulette is generally better because its house edge is 2.70%, compared with 5.26% for American roulette.

Should I play inside or outside bets?

Inside bets offer larger payouts but lower hit frequency. Outside bets hit more often with lower payouts. Choose based on volatility preference, not expected edge.

Is roulette negative EV?

Yes. In standard roulette variants, expected value is negative for players over long samples.
