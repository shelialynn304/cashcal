How bankroll simulation works

Every casino game has a mathematical advantage called the house edge. This is the percentage the casino expects to keep over time.

Even blackjack, which can have a relatively low edge when played correctly, still tilts against the player. Over enough hands, that edge becomes more visible.

The simulator uses probability models to estimate what might happen across many sessions, rather than pretending one lucky run proves anything.

Understanding variance

Short-term results are heavily driven by variance. You can win for a while. You can also get buried early. Both happen.

The distribution chart shows how often different outcomes occur, while the session chart shows how a bankroll can rise and fall during play.

Blackjack strategy basics

Blackjack is one of the few casino games where skill matters. The goal is to beat the dealer by getting closer to 21 without going over.

The dealer’s visible card matters a lot. Weak dealer cards often allow you to play more conservatively. Strong dealer cards usually force more aggressive decisions.

That’s why basic strategy matters so much. Good decisions reduce damage. Bad ones speed it up.
