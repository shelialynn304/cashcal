Important interpretation note

These systems only alter bet sizing patterns. They do not alter wheel probabilities or payouts.

  • Flat: simplest and most stable stake size.
  • Martingale: high recovery attempts, high crash potential.
  • Fibonacci: slower progression, still escalating exposure.
  • D’Alembert: gradual adjustment, still negative EV.

Bottom line: all remain negative EV in standard roulette.

Beginner strategy takeaway

Roulette systems are risk-shaping tools, not edge-creating tools. They can make outcomes feel different in short runs, but cannot flip expected value positive.

  • Use small base bets if you want slower bankroll decay.
  • Avoid chase behavior after losses.
  • Set strict session stop-loss and stop-win limits before starting.

Roulette strategy FAQ

Can any progression system remove house edge?

No. Progression systems only change stake sizing. The house edge comes from wheel structure and payout schedule.

Why compare strategies if all are negative EV?

Because variance and bust risk still matter for session planning. This helps players understand risk before wagering.

Should I treat this as gambling advice?

No. This is educational simulation content. It does not provide financial, legal, or gambling advice.
