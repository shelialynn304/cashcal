How casino bankroll simulation works

Every casino game has a mathematical advantage called the house edge. That edge represents the percentage of each bet the casino expects to keep over time.

Even games with relatively low house edge, like blackjack, still grind players down if bet sizing and decisions are bad enough.

The simulator uses probability models to estimate what might happen across many sessions instead of pretending one lucky run means anything important.

How long will $200 last?

That depends mostly on bet size and game odds. Betting $5 per round gives a bankroll far more room to survive than betting $20 per round like a maniac.

Larger bets increase variance, shorten survival time, and raise bust risk fast. That is not a theory. That is math doing what math does.

Use the calculator above to compare small-bet and big-bet scenarios and watch confidence die in real time.

Understanding variance

Short-term gambling results are heavily influenced by randomness. Winning streaks and losing streaks happen even when the underlying math stays the same.

Luck can dominate short sessions. Long term, the house edge usually wins. The distribution and session charts help make that painfully clear.

Bankroll strategy basics

One of the fastest ways to self-destruct is betting too large a percentage of your bankroll on each hand, spin, or round.

Smaller bets generally mean longer survival, lower volatility, and a lower chance of getting vaporized early.
