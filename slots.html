How Slot Machines Actually Work

Modern slots use a random number generator (RNG). Every spin gets a new random outcome the moment you press spin. That means spins are independent: the machine does not remember the last result and is never “due” to pay you back.

A losing streak does not increase your chance of a win on the next spin. The next spin is still a fresh random event.

What Affects Your Chances?

RTP (Return to Player)

RTP is the long-run percentage of wagered money returned to players.

In real play: higher RTP can help over many spins, but short sessions can still lose quickly.

Volatility

Volatility describes payout swing size and frequency.

In real play: high volatility means fewer wins, but some wins are much larger.

Number of Paylines

More paylines can create more ways to hit combinations.

In real play: total bet often rises with active lines, so bankroll drains faster.

Bet Size

Bet size scales how fast your bankroll moves up or down.

In real play: bigger bets can create bigger wins and faster busts in the same session.

Bonus Features

Free spins, multipliers, and bonus rounds shift where RTP comes from.

In real play: base game may feel dry while bonus rounds carry much of the return.

RTP vs Reality

RTP is long-term math, not a promise for a short session. You can hit big early or burn through your bankroll fast. That’s variance, not a broken machine.
